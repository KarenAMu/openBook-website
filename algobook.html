Write your own proofs using LaTeX

Bellow is a proof for an equation that producess the sum of the first k numbers $$ \begin{align} T(0) &= \frac{0\cdot (0+1)}{2} = 0 && \text{Basis Step}\\ T(k) &= \frac{k\cdot (k+1)}{2} && \text{Inductive Hypothesis} \\ T(k+1) &= \frac{(k+1)(k+2)}{2} && \text{Inductive Step} \\ &= T(k) + k + 1 && \text{From Lemma 1}\\ &= \frac{k \cdot (k+1)}{2} + k+1 && \text{From I.H}\\ &= \frac{k \cdot (k+1)}{2} + \frac{2(k+1)}{2} \\ &= \frac{k^2 + k + 2k + 2}{2} \\ &= \frac{k^2 + 3k + 2}{2} \\ &= \frac{(k+1)(k+2)}{2} \end{align} $$

Here is how we did it!

\begin{align}
      T(0)    &= \frac{0\cdot (0+1)}{2} = 0      && \text{Basis Step}\\
      T(k)    &= \frac{k\cdot (k+1)}{2}          && \text{Inductive Hypothesis} \\
      T(k+1)  &= \frac{(k+1)(k+2)}{2}            && \text{Inductive Step} \\
      &= T(k) + k + 1              && \text{From Lemma 1}\\
      &= \frac{k \cdot (k+1)}{2} + k+1   && \text{From I.H}\\ 
      &= \frac{k \cdot (k+1)}{2} + \frac{2(k+1)}{2} \\
      &= \frac{k^2 + k + 2k + 2}{2} \\
      &= \frac{k^2 + 3k + 2}{2} \\
      &= \frac{(k+1)(k+2)}{2}
\end{align}
